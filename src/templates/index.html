Rangers Farm System

Tracking the rebuild

The Texas Rangers are rebuilding in earnest. A look at the next wave of talent that the club hopes will lead it back to glory.

By Evan Grant | Staff Writer

Published April 6, 2019

Updated

    The Rangers are starting to fast track relievers.

    Over the course of the last week, three hard-throwing relievers – Peter Fairbanks, DeMarcus Evans and Joe Barlow – all moved to Double-A or higher. While none are ranked in the Dallas Morning News’ top 10 Rangers prospects, that is mostly because prospect lists are usually reserved for everyday players and starting pitchers due to their perceived “impact.”

    Nevertheless, all three could have an impact on the big league team before the end of the season. Fairbanks, 25, is a two-time recipient of Tommy John surgery and probably needs to be fast-tracked simply because he may have limited “bullets” in his arm. The Rangers have now twice moved him up, sending him to Triple-A Nashville this week.

    Fairbanks throws 100 mph. Over the course of his brief stint at Double A Frisco, he struck out 14 and walked none in 7.1 scoreless innings.

    Meanwhile, Evans and Barlow, both of whom are going to Frisco, combined for 24.1 scoreless innings at Class A Down East in May.

    The lower levels of the system are where the future cornerstone pieces of the franchise currently reside, but the Rangers may start to show some results in the bullpen before the end of 2019.
