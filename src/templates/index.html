Rangers Farm System

Tracking the rebuild

The Texas Rangers are rebuilding in earnest. A look at the next wave of talent that the club hopes will lead it back to glory.

By Evan Grant | Staff Writer

Published April 6, 2019

Updated

    There is only one infielder in our current list of the Rangers Top 10 prospects.

    That could soon change.

    The Rangers could quickly be looking at a glut of third basemen. With top pick Josh Jung expected to sign as soon as Texas Tech is done in the College World Series and second-pick Davis Wendzel likely to be done at the same time, the Rangers are bringing two highly-regarded third basemen into the system. Jung, on pure upside alone, will likely crack our top 10 as soon as his name is on a contract. It’s likely Wendzel could end up switching positions once he is in the Rangers’ farm system; second base is a possibility.

    But there are others. A quick rundown:

    Jonathan Ornelas: The Rangers’ third-round selection a year ago out of high school in suburban Phoenix had a strong rookie league season and is backing it up with impressive play at Class A Hickory despite having just turned 19 at the end of May. Though he’s hit a slump at the plate in June, he’s shown really good defensive instincts and a mature approach. Ornelas played primarily third base last year in the Arizona Rookie League, but has played all over the diamond this year, with more starts at second base than anywhere else.

    Sherten Apostel: The reason Ornelas has played second is so that the Apostel, 20, can play primarily third base at Hickory. Apostel was acquired from Pittsburgh in the Keone Kela deal last July. A native of Curacao, Apostel had a slow April, but has been on the upswing ever since. He hit .282 with a .791 OPS in May and has followed it up with a .306/.422/.750/1.172 June. He hit five of his nine homers after June 1.

    Diosbel Arias: Soon to turn 23, Arias may be a bit old for Class A Down East, but he has shown he can hit. He carried a .339/.417/.450/.867 slash line into the week. Arias is more hitter than power hitter, which might cap his ceiling, but if he develops a bit more power, he could have a higher prospect profile. His strike out rate has risen significantly this season, so that will also be important to monitor.

    A position that looked like it might be barren when Adrian Beltre retired last fall could very quickly be busting at the seams.
