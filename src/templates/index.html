Rangers Farm System

Tracking the rebuild

The Texas Rangers are rebuilding in earnest. A look at the next wave of talent that the club hopes will lead it back to glory.

By Gerry Fraley | Staff Writer

Published April 6, 2019

    If the Rangers are to make something of this rebuilding project, they have to develop talent from within the organization. Clubs that have gone this route, such as Atlanta a generation ago, Houston and the Chicago Cubs, rebuilt their talent pipelines before dabbling in free agency.

    General manager Jon Daniels put the club in better position to do that with veterans-for-prospects trades last summer. The next step is for the system to produce major-league talent again.

    “We have a chance to be viewed as top 10 system a year from now,” Daniels said. “We’ve got to go out and earn it. The player development group has a lot of work to do to get these guys prepared.”

    Through conversations with Rangers officials and talent evaluations with other organizations, The Dallas Morning News put together a list of the top 10 individual prospects. This list will be re-evaluated each month as the season progresses, with weekly updates to come.
